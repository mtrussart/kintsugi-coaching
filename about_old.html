A Few Words About Us

Certified organisational Coach 0 Years

I have been working as a senior post-doctoral researcher in Bioinformatics at the Walter and Eliza Hall Research Institute of Medical Science in Melbourne during the past 3 years. Whilst throughout my engineering degree in applied mathematics and my PhD degree in Biomedecine, I have been able to develop my knowledge and technical skills, I am now keen to be actively involved in promoting women in STEM (science, technology, engineering and mathematics) careers and leadership roles.

I am particularly concern by the low rate of female participation in STEM and STEM careers. I have been aiming to address the low rate of female participation in STEM education and careers by getting involved in several association to empower women and raise awareness and interest of high-school girls. Indeed, I am the vice-president of the R-Ladies Melbourne which is Australia's first R Programming community for women that exists to promote gender diversity. Our organisation was founded in September 2016 and we have reached more than 1300 members. Between 2016 and 2019, we have organised events every month covering a large area of R programming tools and we are pro-actively searching for women programmers that are willing to take the opportunity to present their work in a friendly environment. I recently received Kellaway Excellence awards of education for my involvement in this community. Additionally, I am involved in the BrainSTEM mentoring program of high-school across Victoria. Between March and June 2018, I mentored regional female high school students in the Regional Girls Innovation Challenge to realise a novel science innovation project. More recently I joined the Women in Science Parkville Precinct (WiSPP) which is a collaborative effort across five of Australia’s largest medical research institutes to boost the numbers of women in science leadership. I am part of the individual development group which is organising quality individual development workshops and events aimed at addressing the barriers and enablers for women’s participation and leadership in science.

While acknowledging the importance of offering technical skills workshops with these associations, I realised that there is a need to help women identifying individual factors that contribute to their participation, achievements and career progression. I have been taking part in the Accelerated Leadership Program organised by Women & Leadership Australia. For my project in this program, I realised a survey to assess the self professional-worth to examine how to promote gender equality by identifying other individual factors that could contribute to women’s participation, achievement and progression. Based on the results of this survey and the literature, I started to organise coaching sessions aiming to help more women identify the factors and limitations of their career progression and empower them to achieve their professional goals. As a certified organisational coach from IECL organisation, I want to make a difference and I am really enthusiastic about my role as a coach as I feel it is helping me achieving one of my long-term goal which is to help empowering women and provide development opportunities for successful leadership in STEM professions and strategies to grow and thrive.
